About me

I am a PhD student in Neuroscience at New York University, and currently a student in Dr. Paul Glimcher's lab . I received my BA in Psychology from UC Berkeley in 2019 and compeleted a two-year postbac fellowship at NIH before starting at NYU.

My research interest is mainly about the neural mechanisms underlying decision making process. Specifically, I am interested in individuals' differences regarding similar decisions. Currently I am working on projects involving Divisive Normalization model and how information efficiency plays a role in making decision.
